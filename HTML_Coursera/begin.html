Socio Demographic Situation of the Aged Population in Bangladesh

Population Sciences

Introduction:

The proportion of the aged population in Bangladesh is higher than ever and it has been increasing day by day.

Socio-demographic situation of the aged population in Bangladesh:

The increasing number of the aged population resulted from demographic transition in Bangladesh has created a complex socio-demographic situation of elderly population in the country. To understand the socio-demographic situation of this portion of the population it is essential to go through the different socio-demographic situation of the elderly population. Hence, in my paper I am going to describe the socio-demographic situation of the aged population in Bangladesh through the discussion of the following variables.

Size and Structure:

By the year 2019 the number of aged population (60 years old and above) in Bangladesh has surpassed 13 million, which is predicted to be over 36 million by the year 2050 (AgeingAsia.org, n.d.) The 2011 census data show that the number of 60 years old above male and female is 5724975 and 5044525 where the male elderly to female elderly ratio is 113.4 (Bangladesh Bureau of Statistics, 2012). The portion of aged people in the population is 7.9% (Bangladesh Bureau of Statistics, 2019).

Distribution:

Age distribution of elderly regarding male and female in Bangladesh is very similar. Among the age groups of elderly, 60-64 year age group possess the bulk percentage of total elderly population with 37% of elderly belong to the age group, where 65-69, 70-74 and 75+ age group possess respectively 21, 20 and 22 percent of total elderly in the country (Bangladesh Bureau of Statistics, 2015).
